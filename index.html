This page offers interactive charts that enable exploration of economic indicators across European Union and Post-Soviet nations.
Through a range of visualizations, users can compare the differences in economic performance among these countries and
gain insights into their development over time.

Section 1: GDP amongst Post-Soviet and European Union members

This chart displays Gross Domestic Product values in millions of dollars. Using the country selector, we can choose between various countries. Additionally, the chart includes a slider for selecting preferred date ranges. These two filters enable us to analyze more than two countries and various time periods.

Section 2: Economic Output of ISIC Sectors in Post-Soviet and EU Member Countries

This chart visualizes the combined economic output of ISIC sectors across various countries. The values are displayed in millions of dollars. The country selector allows us to compare different countries' economic performances, while the slider enables selection of specific date ranges. Also, we have a tooltip that appears when hovering over the bars, explaining which ISIC sector each bar represents.

Section 3: Exports and Imports of Post-Soviet and EU Member Countries

This chart visualizes the values of exports and imports in millions of dollars across Post-Soviet and EU member countries. Users can compare trade performances among different countries using the country selector. Additionally, the slider enables selection of specific date ranges.Also for more precise values there is a tooltip which shows us the exact aggregated sum of values if we hover upon the bars.

Section 4: GDP vs. Household Expenditure: A Comparison of Post-Soviet and EU Member Countries

This chart compares Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and household expenditure in millions of dollars across Post-Soviet and EU member countries. It highlights the economic disparities and consumer spending patterns between these two groups of nations. The country selector allows for specific country comparisons, while the interactive nature of the chart facilitates analysis over different time periods.

Section 5: Changes in Inventories Across Post-Soviet and EU Member Countries

This chart visualizes the changes in inventories across Post-Soviet and EU member countries. The values are displayed in millions of dollars, reflecting fluctuations in stored goods over time. Users can use the country selector to compare inventory changes among different nations. Additionally, the slider allows for selection of specific date ranges, enabling detailed analysis of inventory trends over varying periods.
